4: Anti-aliasing



In the spatial high pass image, pronounced alaiasing occurs on the right zebra's forehead and nose, and in the center where the left (viewer's left) zebra's leg bulge is. In general there is aliasing along the zebra's stripes since there aren't enough pixels to hold this information In the frequency domain high pass image, the high frequency components away from the centre are corrupted since the frequency signals overlap with each other and the resultant wave has a higher amplitude than the original signal (hence more bright spots/ relatively higher frequencies away from center compared to the orignal frequency representation)

The low pass image remains almost the same with some aliasing around the sunglasses and the lower rocks Similar results can be seen in the frequency domain of this image where it looks similar to the frequency domain representation of the original image as the bright segments remain concentrated around the center



Heavy aliasing can be observed at almost every stripe boundary in the high pass image. The frequency representation shows similar results since almost all the high frequency components are corrupted as compared to the original frequency represnetation owing to the same reason as before. Only the birghtness (high frequencies) around the center cluster are properly preserved

Some aliasing can also be observed in the low pass image this time although not as much as the high pass image. The frequency represnetation tells a similar story wherein it looks fairly similar to the orignal frequency domain illustration but a bit more noisy away from the center (due to the same overlapping!)



The half size subsampled image used a Gaussian filter with standard deviation 0.75 while the quarter subsampled image used standard deviation of 1.25. Both were filtered using a Gaussian filter of size 17

In the anti-aliased half subsampled image edges (high frequency components) are much smoother compared to the non anti-aliased version which has jagged edges. The low frequency components remain the same since they are mostly unaffected by the low-pass Gaussian filter. Overall the ant-aliased image is much better The quarter subsampled image shows similar results with smoother high frequency components (edges) thanks to our Gaussian filter and mostly unchanged low frequency components. However, this image is much smaller than the original and does not store much information and does not look as good but far better than the non anti-aliased one.
